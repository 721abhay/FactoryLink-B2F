2. WHERE TO FIND FACTORIES — EVERY METHOD

Finding the right factories is not hard if you know where to look. The mistake most people make is searching
online only. The best factory connections happen physically and personally. Here are all methods:


METHOD 1 Physical
Visit to Industrial Area

Every city in India has one or more industrial areas or MIDC zones. Just go there and
walk around. You will see small factory signboards everywhere. Note down names,
products they make, and owner contacts. Best cities for clothing: Tirupur, Surat,
Ludhiana, Delhi Okhla, Mumbai Dharavi. For groceries: visit your city APMC market —
this is where rice mills, flour mills, oil factories all operate.


METHOD 2 IndiaMART

Go to indiamart.com. Search your product category plus city — example 'cotton t-shirt
manufacturer Mumbai' or 'rice mill Pune'. You get a list of verified manufacturers with
phone numbers, email IDs, and addresses. Filter by 'verified supplier' badge for more
reliable contacts. This is the fastest way to get 20-30 factory contacts in one hour from
your desk.


METHOD 3 Government
MSME Udyam Portal

Website: udyamregistration.gov.in — free government database of all registered small
manufacturers. Search by NIC code (industry code) and pin code. Every factory
registered here has their address, product category, and contact details. These are
verified legal businesses. Best source for finding factories nobody else is approaching
yet.


METHOD 4 Trade Fairs
and Exhibitions

CMAI Garment Fair (Mumbai and Delhi) — hundreds of small clothing manufacturers in
one place. AAHAAR International Food and Hospitality Fair — food and grocery
manufacturers. India Food Expo — direct food processors and mills. Attending one
trade fair gives you 50 factory owner contacts, business cards, and face to face
conversations in one single day.


METHOD 5 Trace Back
from Wholesale Market

Go to your nearest wholesale market. Buy something — any product in a category you
want on your platform. Ask the wholesaler very simply: 'Which company makes this? I
want to place a large direct order.' They will tell you or show you the brand. Then search
that brand on IndiaMART or Google. Within 2-3 conversations you reach the actual
factory owner directly.


METHOD 6 Personal
Network and Referrals

Ask everyone in your personal network — family, friends, former colleagues, college
batchmates. Do you know anyone who owns or manages a small factory or knows
someone who does? One warm introduction from a mutual contact is worth 50 cold
visits. The first 5-10 factory partners you sign should ideally come from personal
referrals. They trust you faster and give you better terms because of the personal
relationship.




METHOD 7 LinkedIn
and JustDial Business

Search LinkedIn for 'production manager' or 'factory owner' in your city plus product
category. Connect and send a short direct message explaining your platform. JustDial
Business section also lists manufacturing units with direct phone numbers. These
methods are slower but can reach factory owners that IndiaMART does not list.

team calls factory to discuss quality issue before more orders are sent.

Factory profile now visible to your order routing algorithm. When group
orders form in their area for their product category — orders start arriving.
Factory sees new order notification on their dashboard. They confirm or
decline within 2 hours. Production begins. Platform tracks progress.
Payment released on delivery confirmation.

10. HOW FACTORY AND CUSTOMER CONNECT — THE FULL FLOW

This is the complete journey showing exactly how a customer order reaches a factory and comes back as a
delivered product. This happens automatically inside the app once both sides are registered.


STEP 1

Customer Browses and
Joins Group Order

Customer opens app. Sees product listing with factory price, retail
comparison, timer, zone progress bar. Joins order. Pays 30% advance

STEP 2

App Groups Nearby Orders

App continuously monitors all orders in same zone for same product.
When zone hits minimum quantity OR timer expires — whichever

STEP 3

App Routes Order to
Nearest Verified Factory

Smart matching engine checks which verified factory in that area has

Factory owner gets push notification: New bulk order — 28 t-shirts size

Factory produces the order. Updates status in app: In production, then

Courier Picks Up from

Your courier partner receives dispatch notification automatically. Picks

STEP 7

Courier follows optimized route. Delivers to anchor points and colonies
on the route. Anchor point manager scans arrival. Customers notified:
Your order has arrived at [anchor point name]. For home delivery:

STEP 8

Customer Collects and
Confirms

Customer goes to anchor point. Scans QR code on their order.
Package released to them. App prompts: Rate your order quality — 1
to 5 stars. On confirmation — remaining 70% payment released to
factory within 48 hours automatically.




11. REAL PROBLEMS IN FACTORY-CUSTOMER CONNECTION AND
SOLUTIONS

n Factory declines order after accepting


THE PROBLEM

THE SOLUTION

Factory confirms order then calls to say they cannot fulfill
due to material shortage or machine breakdown.

Build a mandatory 2 hour confirmation window. Once
factory taps Accept — a cancellation penalty applies if
they withdraw within 24 hours of production start. Also
always have Factory B ready. Order automatically
reroutes to backup factory within 30 minutes of any
cancellation. Customer never waits.

n Customer collection rate at anchor point is low


THE PROBLEM

THE SOLUTION

Orders arrive at anchor point but 20-30% of customers
do not collect within 2 days. Anchor point gets
congested. Factory payment gets delayed.

Automated reminders at 6 hours, 24 hours, and 48 hours
after arrival notification. After 48 hours uncollected —
customer is charged a small storage fee per day. After 5
days uncollected — order returned and full refund minus
logistics cost. This trains customers to collect promptly
within 2 deliveries.

n Factory and customer dispute over quality


THE PROBLEM

THE SOLUTION

Customer says quality is bad. Factory says customer is
lying or being unreasonable. You are caught in the
middle.

Photo evidence mandatory from customer within 24
hours of collection. Your team reviews photo
independently within 4 hours. Clear quality standards
documented for each product category and shared with
factory at onboarding. Decision made by your team is
final and binding on both parties. Factory knew this rule
before joining.



n Factory wants to deal directly with customer after meeting through your platform


THE PROBLEM

THE SOLUTION

After a few orders factory owner gets customer contact
details and tries to bypass your platform to avoid your
platform fee.

Customer contact details are never shared with factory
— ever. Factory sees order number and product details
only. Never customer name, phone, or address directly.
Delivery address goes only to courier — not to factory.
Any factory found attempting direct contact with
customers is permanently removed from platform.

n New factory has no quality score and customers are hesitant


THE PROBLEM

THE SOLUTION

Customer sees a factory with no ratings and prefers to
wait for a rated factory. New factory never gets first
orders. Cannot build rating. Catch 22.

New factories start with a platform guaranteed badge
instead of customer rating badge. Your team has
physically verified them. Platform puts its own reputation
behind them. First 5 orders of any new factory are also
offered at a small additional discount to attract early
adopters willing to try a new factory. First customers to
rate a new factory get bonus wallet credit as thank you.

WHATSAPP MESSAGE — First contact after finding on IndiaMART


Hello [Owner Name], I got your contact from IndiaMART. My name is [Your Name] and I am building a
platform called FactoryLink that gives small manufacturers in [City] guaranteed monthly bulk orders with
payment in 48 hours — no 30-60 day credit like wholesalers. I would like to discuss if we can work
together. Are you available for a 10 minute call this week?

WHATSAPP MESSAGE — Follow up after no response in 3 days


Hello [Owner Name], following up on my message from [day]. I understand you are busy with production.
I just wanted to share one thing quickly — we currently have [number] customers in [area near their
factory] who are looking for a local [product type] manufacturer for a recurring monthly order. If timing
works, I would love to explore if we are a good fit. No pressure at all.

EMAIL SUBJECT LINE AND BODY — For factories with email listed on IndiaMART


SUBJECT: Guaranteed monthly bulk orders for your factory — FactoryLink Partnership Dear [Owner
Name], I am reaching out because I found your factory on IndiaMART and believe we can work together
well. I am building FactoryLink — a platform that groups together orders from 20-50 customers in your
city and places confirmed bulk orders directly with local manufacturers like yourself. What we offer you:
— Guaranteed minimum orders every month including your slow season — 30% payment before
production starts, 70% within 48 hours of delivery — No middlemen — your factory name and product
shown directly to customers — Real customer feedback on your products I would like to visit your factory
for 30 minutes this week to discuss this further. Please reply to this email or WhatsApp me at [Your
Number] to schedule a time. Best regards, [Your Name] FactoryLink

IN-PERSON INTRODUCTION — When visiting industrial area cold


Hello, I am [Your Name] from FactoryLink. We are building a platform that connects local factories directly
to customers in this city — cutting out wholesalers completely so factories get better margins and
customers get lower prices. I am meeting factory owners in this area today. Is the owner available for 10
minutes? I just have three quick questions about your production capacity.
