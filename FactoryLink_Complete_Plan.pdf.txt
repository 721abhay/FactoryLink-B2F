2. EVERY REAL PROBLEM AND ITS SOLUTION

PROBLEM 1 — Customer Trust and Upfront Payment


THE PROBLEM

THE SOLUTION

You are asking people to pay money before receiving
product. Indian customers especially in tier 2-3 cities do
not trust this. One bad experience spreads to 10 people
immediately.

Start with split payment: 30% at booking, 70% just
before delivery. Or wallet model for subscription
customers. Never ask 100% upfront until trust is fully
established.

PROBLEM 2 — Timer Fails Without Enough Customers


THE PROBLEM

THE SOLUTION

Your model depends on enough people joining a group
order in 24-48 hours. Early days you have very few
customers. Timer expires again and again without hitting
minimum. Factories lose confidence in you.

In early days personally fill the gap. If 8 people ordered
and minimum is 15 — buy remaining 7 yourself and
absorb cost. Painful but necessary to build reliability and
factory trust.

PROBLEM 3 — Factory Quality Becomes Your Reputation


THE PROBLEM

THE SOLUTION

When customer gets bad quality product they blame
YOUR app not the factory. You have taken on reputation
risk of every factory on your platform. One viral
complaint can kill you early.

Hire even one quality checker to visit factories and check
sample batches before dispatch. Show factory
verification card in app with real photos, owner face, and
quality score from past orders.

PROBLEM 4 — Zone Minimum Orders Takes Too Long to Fill


THE PROBLEM

THE SOLUTION

Zones need 25-30 orders to justify a delivery run. Early
months you may have only 3-4 orders per zone per
week. Customer waits 2 weeks and never comes back.

Forget zone minimum in the beginning. Do manual
deliveries yourself even at a loss. Only introduce zone
minimum system once you have enough regular
customers to fill zones consistently.

PROBLEM 5 — 3 Month Commitment Scares Customers


THE PROBLEM

THE SOLUTION

Asking someone to commit grocery money for 3 months
upfront is a major psychological barrier. What if they
move? What if quality is bad? Most will say no even if the
saving is good.

Start with 1 month subscription only. Prove quality and
reliability first. Then offer 3 month option as an upgrade
with extra discount for committed customers only.



PROBLEM 6 — Small Factories Have Inconsistent Supply


THE PROBLEM

THE SOLUTION

Small local factories run out of raw material, have
machine breakdowns, cannot fulfill on time. Unlike big
factories they have no backup systems. Your customer
suffers and blames you.

Always maintain 2 factory partners for every product
category. Never depend on one factory. If one fails the
other covers automatically. Build dual factory backup
routing into the app logic.

PROBLEM 7 — Dangerous Cash Flow Gap


THE PROBLEM

THE SOLUTION

You collect customer money then pay factory then pay
logistics. In between is a timing gap. Refunds, delays,
quality rejections can drain your cash fast even when
business looks profitable on paper.

Never release full payment to factory until delivery is
confirmed by customer. Hold 20-30% back as security.
Use escrow — money releases only when customer
confirms receipt.

PROBLEM 8 — Anchor Points Have Seasonal Gaps


THE PROBLEM

THE SOLUTION

Colleges are empty 3-4 months every year. Offices go
thin during festivals. Your most reliable delivery zones
suddenly disappear for weeks disrupting order volumes.

Plan product calendar around this. Push grocery
subscriptions during college months. Push festival
special orders during thin office periods. Always have
multiple anchor point types per zone.

PROBLEM 9 — GST Eats Into Your Promised Savings


THE PROBLEM

THE SOLUTION

GST does not disappear just because you removed the
middleman. Factory charges GST. You charge customer
GST. Logistics also has GST. Your 50% saving promise
becomes 35% in reality.

Be transparent with customers from day one. Show the
full breakup — factory price, GST, logistics fee. Never
promise 50% if actual saving is 35%. Trust only breaks
when reality differs from the promise.

PROBLEM 10 — Retailers and Wholesalers Will Fight Back


THE PROBLEM

THE SOLUTION

When you start taking their customers, traditional
middlemen will pressure factories not to work with you.
They will spread negative word of mouth and temporarily
undercut prices to stop your growth.

Your protection is speed of growth and customer loyalty.
The faster you grow the less any middleman can do.
Also target small factories that have no existing
relationship with major wholesalers yet.



PROBLEM 11 — Different Products Come From Different Factories


THE PROBLEM

THE SOLUTION

Colony delivery: Person A ordered rice from flour mill,
Person B ordered t-shirts from garment factory. These
come from completely different factories at different
times. Combining into one delivery is complex.

Use route-based delivery not product-based. Delivery
route passes through all stops regardless of which
factory. One small exception handling room needed —
not a warehouse. Just for consolidation and failed
deliveries.

PROBLEM 12 — Not Enough Orders Per Office or College Zone


THE PROBLEM

THE SOLUTION

4-5 people ordering from one office is not enough to
justify a dedicated delivery to that single location. Too
few orders per anchor point makes logistics unprofitable.

Zone clustering: group all anchor points within 1-2 km
into one delivery zone. Zone needs combined 25-30
orders. One truck covers all stops in one circular route
pass. The app shows zone progress — customers share
app with colleagues to fill zone and unlock delivery.

4. DELIVERY AND LOGISTICS MODEL

FactoryLink never holds stock. Product goes directly from factory to customer. You are a connection platform
not a storage business. This is your biggest cost advantage over every competitor.


COMPETITORS — Hold Inventory

FACTORYLINK — Zero Inventory

Blinkit, BigBasket, Amazon: dark stores in every area,
massive rent, staff to manage stock, capital locked in
unsold products, wastage from expired items. All costs
passed to customer.

Factory holds all stock. No rent. No warehouse staff. No
capital locked. No wastage. Only costs are platform
development and delivery coordination. Profitable at
much smaller scale.


ANCHOR POINTS

Fixed collection spots where people already go daily — college gates, office buildings,
school gates, market areas. Security guard or canteen owner manages collections for
small fee of Rs.500-1000 per month. Very low cost.


ZONE CLUSTERING

All anchor points within 1-2 km radius form one delivery zone. Zone needs minimum
25-30 orders before dispatch. App shows zone progress bar to customers. Customers
share app with colleagues to fill their zone — free marketing.


ROUTE OPTIMIZATION

Every morning app builds most efficient circular delivery route covering all zones. One
truck enters zone and hits all anchor points. Even colonies with 3-4 orders get served if
on natural route — almost zero extra cost per additional stop.


EXCEPTION HANDLING

99% of orders: factory packs ® courier picks up ® delivers to anchor point ® customer
collects. You never touch product. 1% of orders: delivery failure, quality rejection,
consolidation needed. One small room handles all exceptions. NOT a warehouse.

10. WHAT YOU MUST DO BEFORE BUILDING THE APP


MOST IMPORTANT: Do NOT build the app first. Validate manually. Most startups waste Rs.10-20 lakhs
building before proving anything works. Do these 5 validations first. They cost almost nothing and tell you
everything.


1

WILL PEOPLE
ACTUALLY PAY?

Go talk to 50 families in one colony. In person — not online. Ask ONE
question: Will you give me Rs.500 advance right now for guaranteed
groceries at 40% less delivered monthly? If 10 out of 50 give actual money
— idea is validated. If nobody pays — rethink before building anything.


2

WILL FACTORIES WORK
WITH YOU?

Find one small garment factory or rice mill. Sit with the owner. Show
guaranteed orders from real customers. Ask his lowest bulk price for
confirmed advance volume. If he gives 30% below market — your supply
side works. If every factory says no — your pricing model is broken.


3

WHAT DOES LOGISTICS
ACTUALLY COST?

Before building any route optimization — do one delivery yourself. Hire one
auto for a day. Deliver 20 orders across one zone manually. Calculate
actual cost per order. If logistics costs Rs.40 per order and your margin is
Rs.30 — the business does not work no matter how good the app.


4

WHAT IS YOUR LEGAL
AND GST STRUCTURE?

Before taking any customer money — understand your legal structure.
Marketplace or trader? This changes your entire GST liability. One CA
consultation now saves lakhs in problems later. Non-negotiable.


5

DO YOU HAVE THE
RIGHT TEAM?

This business needs: one person for technology, one for factory
negotiation, one for customer and delivery coordination. Do you have these
three people fully committed? A great idea with a weak team always fails. A
decent idea with a strong committed team always finds a way.
